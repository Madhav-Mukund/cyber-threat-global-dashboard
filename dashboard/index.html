💡 Key Geopolitical Insights

🇨🇳 → 🇺🇸 China–US Cyber Rivalry

The China → USA corridor is the #1 attack corridor globally, dominated by state-sponsored espionage operations targeting government and defense sectors.

🇷🇺 → 🇺🇦 Russia–Ukraine Conflict

Russia's cyber operations against Ukraine have surged since 2022, making it one of the most active attack corridors with high-intensity disruption attacks.

📊 10× Volume Surge

Incidents surged from ~71 in 2020 to 700+ in 2023, driven by the Russia-Ukraine war and expanded tracking — the opposite trend from breach-notification databases.

⚠️ Attribution Gap

~34% of incidents have no confident attribution. Recent years look worse due to recency bias — investigations are still ongoing.

🔴 State Actors Hit Harder

Despite lower volume, state actors have higher median intensity scores than non-state groups, indicating more impactful and sophisticated operations.

💰 Ransomware Explosion

Ransomware has become a dominant attack type since 2020, with dyadic records growing dramatically alongside disruption and data theft operations.
